INTRODUCTION

This document supplements the EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) with internal guidance
specific to our organisation. It is not a public document and must not be shared externally.


SECTION 1 - INTERNAL AI USE CASE CLASSIFICATION

We have classified internal AI use cases into two tiers:

TIER A (High-risk, requires full compliance program):
- CV screening and candidate ranking tools used in recruitment
- Performance evaluation AI assistants used by HR managers
- Internal creditworthiness tools supporting employee benefit decisions
- Any AI integrated into safety-critical internal infrastructure (e.g. data-centre monitoring)

All Tier A tools must undergo internal risk assessment before deployment, complete the
self-assessment template in Appendix 2, and be registered in the internal AI inventory.

TIER B (Lower-risk, streamlined review):
- Document summarisation tools (legal, finance, HR)
- Meeting transcription assistants
- Code review assistants (e.g. GitHub Copilot integrations)
- Customer service chatbots handling FAQ queries only

Tier B tools require a simplified one-page self-assessment and approval from the direct
business unit head. They are still registered in the AI inventory but do not require full
risk management documentation.


SECTION 2 - LEGACY TOOL REMEDIATION TIMELINE

Following the formal entry into force of the EU AI Act in August 2024 and its phased
application schedule, we have defined the following internal remediation deadlines:

Phase 1 (Prohibited practices check) - Deadline: December 31, 2025
All internal AI tools must be reviewed against Article 5 prohibited practices.
Any tool found to use subliminal manipulation or exploit user vulnerabilities must be
immediately suspended pending legal review.

Phase 2 (High-risk classification audit) - Deadline: March 31, 2026
All Tier A tools must have completed the internal risk management documentation.
The AI inventory must be updated with compliance status for each tool.

Phase 3 (Technical documentation and transparency) - Deadline: August 2, 2026
Full compliance documentation must be in place for all tools classified as high-risk
under the EU AI Act (aligning with Article 16 requirements).

Phase 4 (Ongoing monitoring) - From August 3, 2026
Quarterly compliance reviews for all registered AI tools. Annual external audit for
Tier A tools handling personal data.


SECTION 3 - HR PROCESS GUIDANCE

AI tools used in HR processes are categorised as Tier A due to their potential impact
on employment opportunities (see Annex III of EU AI Act - high-risk categories include
"Employment, workers management and access to self-employment").

Specific rules:
- No AI decision on hiring, promotion or termination may be final without human review.
- Candidates and employees must be informed when AI is used to assess their applications.
- HR AI tools must log decisions and retain logs for at least 3 years.
- Bias testing on HR AI tools must be conducted semi-annually by an independent team.

Current HR AI tools registered in our inventory:
- TalentScreen v2.3 (CV shortlisting) - Tier A - compliance review in progress
- PerfomanceInsights (performance analytics) - Tier A - pending Article 9 risk assessment
